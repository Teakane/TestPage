Trailing Plants

Welcome to our collection of trailing plants!
Embrace the cascading beauty of trailing plants in our selection. These graceful wonders add a touch of whimsy and elegance to any space, with their trailing vines and lush foliage. Elevate your indoor garden with these versatile and enchanting plants, perfect for hanging baskets, shelves, or trailing from tall planters.

Baby's Tears

Soleirolia soleirolii is a flowering plant belonging to the nettle family. It has a number of common names, including baby's tears.

Sunlight: Gentle, indirect light.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Baby's tears loves high humidity and bright, indirect light, but it will tolerate lower light levels. Keep this plant slightly moist at all times. If it dries out, it will quickly decline. Water the plant from beneath by placing it in a sink of water and letting it soak up water through the pot's drainage holes.

Chenille Plant

Acalypha hispida, the chenille plant, is a flowering shrub which belongs to the Acalypha genus. It is the fourth largest genus of its family, and is native to Hawaii and Oceania.

Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade. (but avoid late afternoon direct sunlight)

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
The chenille plant needs to be kept moist but not wet and should never be allowed to dry out completely. If possible, keep water off the flowers in order to prevent disease. Plant in a good general purpose potting medium. Fertilize container-grown plants frequently, as this is a heavy feeder when forming flowers.

English Ivy

English ivy (Hedera helix), a vigorous and clinging evergreen vine native to Europe and western Asia, is renowned for its ornamental beauty and resilience, often found adorning walls, fences, and tree trunks in gardens, waste spaces, and wild habitats. This plant has become naturalized in many areas outside its native range, thriving without human assistance and holding significant cultural symbolism.

Sunlight: Bright indirect light.

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
The English ivy likes moist soil but hates being soggy. It is recommended to check the soil regularly, and to water when the top two inches feel dry. It loves humidity too, so give it a spritz with a mister every other day. Ivies also enjoy an occasional shower, to wash dirt and debris off their leaves.

Grape Ivy

Cissus alata, known colloquially as grape ivy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Vitaceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of the New World, ranging from Mexico to Bolivia and extending into Venezuela, Trinidad, and Guyana.

Sunlight: At least 6 hours of bright southern sunlight per day.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Water when the soil is almost completely dry, and mist every few days to stop the leaf edges from turning brown.

Hoya Linearis

Hoya linearis is a flowering plant species within the Hoya genus, originating from Nepal and China. It is recognized for its unique foliage characterized by narrow, cylindrical leaves measuring 1–2 inches (25–51 mm) in length.

Sunlight: Bright but indirect filtered light.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Hoya linearis is known as a difficult plant to care for. Place it in bright, indirect light, allowing the top inch of soil to dry between waterings, maintaining moderate temperatures, providing high humidity, using well-draining soil. Fertilize monthly during the growing season, provide support for vining stems, repot every 2-3 years, and monitor for pests to ensure optimal growth and health.

Peperomia

Peperomia is one of the two large genera of the family Piperaceae. It is estimated that there are at least over 1,000 species, occurring in all tropical and subtropical regions of the world.

Sunlight: Medium to bright indirect light.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Peperomia leaves may fall off because of a lack of light, underwatering, or overwatering. Ensure your plant gets plenty of indirect light and allow the soil to dry slightly between waterings and then water thoroughly. Also, ensure your peperomia has adequate drainage so it doesn't get waterlogged and develop root rot.

Philodendron

Philodendron is a large genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae, containing over 450 plants.

Sunlight: Bright indirect sunlight.

Pet friendly: No! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Avoid afternoon sun, especially in summer, as leaves may sunburn. Leaves remain on the plant for a long period, you don't want them marred by scalded spots. Keep water away from the rosettes and removing dead leaves from the bottom. Water at the soil level to avoid splashing the leaves.

Spider Plant

Chlorophytum comosum, colloquially known as the spider plant, is a species of evergreen perennial flowering plant of the family Asparagaceae. It is native to tropical and Southern Africa but has become naturalized in other parts of the world, including Western Australia and Bangladesh.

Sunlight: Bright to moderate indirect sunlight.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Water your spider plant when 50-75% of the soil volume is dry. Dry crispy tips often point to underwatering while dark brown tips point to overwatering. Water thoroughly and discard of any excess water in the saucer.

String of Hearts

Ceropegia woodii, known as the string of hearts, is a flowering plant belonging to the dogbane family Apocynaceae, originating from South Africa, Eswatini, and Zimbabwe.

Sunlight: Bright, indirect light.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Grow string of hearts in bright indirect light, out of direct sunshine. Water regularly in spring and summer, allowing the soil to completely dry out between waterings. Reduce watering in winter, and never let the plant sit in water as the roots can rot and kill the plant.

Tradescantia

Tradescantia is a genus comprising 85 species of herbaceous perennial wildflowers within the Commelinaceae family, native to the Americas spanning from southern Canada to northern Argentina, as well as the West Indies.

Sunlight: Bright, indirect light.

Pet friendly: Depends on the pet! While the tradescantia is safe for dogs, it is toxic to cats.

Care and Other:
Tradescantia plants like to be kept moist but not waterlogged. Water your plant once about half the soil has dried out between waterings. Expect to water more often in brighter light and less often in lower light. During the summer months, you may need to water your plant more frequently than during the Winter months. Do not let the soil dry out completely as this can lead to wilting and leaf drop, and avoid overwatering.
