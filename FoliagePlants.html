Foliage Plants

Welcome to our collection of foliage houseplants!
Here, leaves steal the spotlight with their diverse shapes, colors, and textures.
Discover our selection of foliage plants and unleash your creativity to transform your space with vibrant greenery.

Banana Tree

The Banana Tree is a tropical plant prized for its bold foliage and, in some cases, its edible fruit. Despite its name, the Banana Tree is not a tree but a large herbaceous plant.

Sunlight: Full sun with shade in the afternoon

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Plant in well-draining, fertile soil rich in organic matter. Keep the soil moist, watering deeply. Water your banana plant less often in the winter months. Remove dead leaves regularly.

Bird of Paradise

The Bird of Paradise Plant is a stunning tropical plant admired for its bold, exotic foliage and vibrant, bird-like flowers. Originating from South Africa, it is a popular choice for adding a touch of the tropics to indoor spaces around the world.

Sunlight: Bright indirect light

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Plant in a peat-based potting mix with perlite or sand to improve drainage. Repot every 2-3 years. Water every 7-10 days and protect from drafts.

Cast Iron Plant

The Cast Iron Plant is a resilient and low-maintenance evergreen plant admired for its glossy, dark green foliage and ability to thrive in low light conditions. Native to East Asia, it is renowned for its durability and adaptability to a wide range of growing conditions, making it an excellent choice for beginner gardeners.

Sunlight: Low indirect light

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Plant in a soil right in organic content. Water sparingly, allowing the soil to dry out first. It is suitable for indoor spaces with dry air.

Dragon Tree

The Dragon Tree is a striking and easy-to-care-for houseplant prized for its architectural form and dramatic foliage. Native to Madagascar and other regions of Africa, it is known for its slender, upright stems adorned with clusters of long, sword-shaped leaves.

Sunlight: Bright indirect light

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Plant in a peat-based mix with perlite or sand. Allow the top inch of soil to dry out before watering. They prefer warm temperatures during the day and cooler temperatures during the night.

Fiber Optic Grass

Fiber Optic Grass is a unique and visually captivating ornamental grass prized for its fine, thread-like foliage that resembles fiber optic strands. Native to New Zealand, Australia, and other regions with temperate climates, it is commonly cultivated as a decorative accent in gardens, landscapes, and indoor containers.

Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Plant in a soil rich in organic matter and keep the soil moist. Protect from cold temperatures.

Money Tree

The Money Tree is a popular and auspicious houseplant believed to bring good luck, prosperity, and positive energy to its surroundings. Native to Central and South America, it is characterized by its braided trunk, glossy green foliage, and distinctive hand-shaped leaves.

Sunlight: Bright indirect light

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Plant in a peat-based potting mix with perlite or sand. Allow the top inch of soil to dry out before watering. They prefer temperatures between 18° and 24° C. Protect your Money Tree from temperature fluctuations.

Monstera

The Monstera is a striking and iconic tropical plant prized for its large, glossy leaves and unique fenestrated foliage. With its dramatic appearance and easy-going nature, the Monstera has become a staple in interior design and indoor gardening.

Sunlight: Bright indirect light

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Plant in soil rich in organic content. Water thoroughly once the top inch of soil has dried out. They require temperatures of 15° C or above but prefer warmer temperatures. Protect from drafts.

Prayer Plant

The Prayer Plant is a charming and distinctive houseplant known for its unique foliage that folds up at night, resembling praying hands, hence its name. Native to the tropical forests of Brazil, it has become a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Sunlight: Bright indirect light

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Plant in a peat based potting mix. Do not waterlog the soil, but ensure that it stays moist and never dries out. They grow well at room temperature, but protect from cold drafts.

Snake Plant

The Snake Plant , also known as Mother-in-Law's Tongue, is prized for its architectural form and low maintenance requirements. Native to West Africa, it has become known for its adaptability to a wide range of growing conditions and its air-purifying properties.

Sunlight: Moderate to low indirect light

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Plant in a mixture of potting mix, perlite, and sand. Repot every 2-3 years. Water sparingly only when the soil has completely dried out.

Ti Plant

The Ti Plant is a tropical ornamental plant admired for its colorful foliage and cultural significance. Native to Southeast Asia, Australia, and the Pacific Islands, it has been cultivated for centuries for its use in traditional medicine, religious ceremonies, and decorative purposes.

Sunlight: Bright indirect light

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Plant in a peat-based potting mix with perlite or sand and repot ever year or two. Keep the soil moist. Protect from temperatures below 15° C and drafts.
