Cacti & Succulents

Welcome to our collection of cacti and succulents!
Explore the unique allure of cacti and succulents in our collection. These resilient beauties bring desert charm and low-maintenance elegance to any space. Discover a variety of shapes, sizes, and colors to add a touch of natural sophistication to your home or office.

Aloe Vera

The aloe vera is a succulent plant species of the genus Aloe. It is widely distributed, and is considered an invasive species in many world regions.

Sunlight: Bright, indirect sunlight needed.

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Aloes need very little water in winter. Aloes are slow growing so repot when the plant has outgrown its pot, usually every two or three years. Feed every couple of months from April to September with a weak plant food. Wipe the leaves occasionally, to prevent dust building up.

Bunny Ears Cactus

Opuntia microdasys (colloquially known as the bunny ears cactus) is a species of flowering plant in the cactus family Cactaceae, native and endemic to central and northern Mexico.

Sunlight: Between 6-8 hours of direct sunlight each day.

Pet friendly: NO! Contains spikes!

Care and Other:
Bunny ear cactus is adapted to receiving less rainfall and is drought-tolerant. While growing in spring & summer, the plant prefers regular watering. Let the soil dry out completely between waterings and avoid overwatering.

Christmas Cactus

Schlumbergera (coloquially known as the christmas cactus) is a compact cactus genus comprising six to nine species native to the coastal mountains of southeastern Brazil. These plants thrive in shaded environments with high humidity, often growing on trees or rocks.

Sunlight: Bright indirect to medium indirect light.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Grow your Christmas cactus in a warm, bright spot out of direct sunshine, and mist a few times a week. In order to flower every year, Schlumbergera need two periods of 'rest' – lower temperatures and less watering – after flowering in late winter and again in September.

Golden Barrel Cactus

Kroenleinia grusonii, commonly referred to as the golden barrel cactus, is a type of barrel cactus native to east-central Mexico.

Sunlight: At least 6 hours of bright southern sunlight per day.

Pet friendly: NO! Contains spikes!

Care and Other:
Golden barrel cactus are prone to root rot. From fall to spring, water infrequently (every month or two) and ensure that the soil drains completely. Do not leave any water in the tray or allow it to sit in water. In winter, simulate drought conditions, giving no water.

Gollum Jade

Crassula ovata, commonly known as the gollum jade, is a succulent plant with small pink or white flowers that is native to southeastern Africa; it is common as a houseplant worldwide.

Sunlight: At least 4-6 hours of sunlight per day.

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
The Gollum jade likes very airy, porous, nutrient-rich soil. Succulents require fast-draining soil that dries completely between waterings. Your soil must have a sandy texture and a low water-holding capacity, just like desert soil.

Hen and Chicks

Hen and chicks is a common name for a group of small rosette-shaped succulent plants, native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

Sunlight: At least 6 hours a day.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Place the plant in bright light, or full sun, unless you live in a very hot, dry climate, then put the plant in light shade. Use well-draining, gravely soil outdoors or succulent/cactus mix indoors. Avoid smothering the plant with too much water and fertilizer. Check the soil for dryness first before watering.

Echeveria Lola

Echeveria is a sizable genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Crassulaceae. These plants are indigenous to semi-desert regions of Central America, Mexico, and northwestern South America.

Sunlight: Bright indirect light.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Avoid afternoon sun, especially in summer, as leaves may sunburn. Leaves remain on the plant for a long period, you don't want them marred by scalded spots. Keep water away from the rosettes and removing dead leaves from the bottom. Water at the soil level to avoid splashing the leaves.

Mistletoe Cactus

Rhipsalis baccifera, known as the mistletoe cactus, is an epiphytic cactus native to Central and South America, the Caribbean, and Florida. It also grows in tropical regions of Africa and extends into Sri Lanka, known as "nawahandi" in Sinhala. Notably, it is the only cactus species naturally occurring outside the Americas.

Sunlight: Bright indirect light.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Prepare well-draining soil and select a pot with drainage holes. Water regularly, allowing the soil to nearly dry out in between, but do not let the plant sit in water. Fertilize lightly during the growing season. Since mistletoe cactus likes humidity, consider placing it near a humidifier or in a bathroom.

Moon Cactus

Gymnocalycium mihanovichii is a cactus species from South America which includes popular chlorophyll-deficient mutants with red, orange, or yellow pigmentation. Commonly grafted onto hylocereus cacti to form "Moon Cacti," they are widely cultivated as houseplants.

Sunlight: Indirect sunlight, with 1 to 2 hours of morning or afternoon light.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Moon Cactus prefers dry environments. Providing extra humidity or misting your plant allows water to linger on leaves, which can create the perfect environment for harmful types of fungi. Moon Cactus is very sensitive to wet soil, so choose a potting soil that drains very well and doesn't retain too much moisture.

Thimble Cactus

Mammillaria vetula, the thimble cactus, is a species of cactus in the subfamily Cactoideae. It is endemic to the Mexican states of Hidalgo, Guanajuato and Querétaro.

Sunlight: Full sun or bright direct light.

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Thimble cactus is a hardy succulent that generally demands minimal maintenance, suitable for beginners. Key to its care is ensuring well-draining soil and ample sunlight, with watering limited to when the soil is completely dry.
