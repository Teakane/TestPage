Flowering Plants

Welcome to our collection of flowering houseplants!
Bring the beauty of nature indoors with these vibrant blooms.
Explore the following options and add a touch of floral elegance to your home today.

African Violet

African violets are small plants that grow well at average room temperature. They flower continuosly.

Sunlight: Partial sun

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
You can use an all-purpose potting soil. Keep them in a small pot and repot every few years. Keep the soil lightly moist with lukewarm water, but do not overwater. Water them from the bottom as the leaves are susceptible to rot.

Anthurium

Anthuriums, also known as flamingo flowers or laceleaf, are prized for their glossy, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spathelike blooms. Originating from tropical regions of Central and South America, these plants add a touch of exotic elegance to any indoor space.

Sunlight: Bright, but undirect light

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Plant in a well-draining, aerated potting mix rich in organic matter, such as a blend of peat moss, perlite, and pine bark. Anthuriums prefer high humidity levels. Mist the leaves regularly or place the plant on a pebble tray filled with water. Do not expose to temperatures below 10° C.

Begonia

Begonias are a diverse group of flowering plants known for their colorful and often intricately patterned foliage, as well as their delicate flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions around the world, Begonias are popular choices for indoor gardens due to their attractive appearance and relative ease of care.

Sunlight: Filtered sunlight or partial shade

Pet friendly: Yes... nontoxic but may cause stomach upset if ingested

Care and Other:
Plant in a well-draining, lightweight potting mix with good aeration. Repot each spring to refresh the soil and provide room for growth. Ensure good air circulation and avoid overhead watering to prevent fungal diseases.

Crown-of-Thorns

Crown-of-Thorns is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar known for its striking appearance and resilient nature. With its thick stems, sharp thorns, and clusters of colorful flowers, Crown-of-Thorns adds a touch of exotic beauty to any indoor or outdoor garden.

Sunlight: Bright direct sunlight, at least 4 hours a day

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested and may cause skin irritation.

Care and Other:
Plant in a cactus or succulent mix. Allow the soil to dry out completely between waterings. Do not expose to temperatures below 10° C.

Geranium

Geraniums, known for their colorful blooms and aromatic foliage, are popular flowering plants cherished for their versatility and resilience. These plants, native to South Africa, come in a variety of colors and forms, making them prized additions to gardens, containers, and indoor spaces.

Sunlight: Partial shade to full sun

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Plant in potting soil mixed with some coarse sand. Allow the soil to dry out slightly between waterings, then water thoroughly until water drains from the bottom of the pot. Avoid exposing your geraniums to temperature fluctuations.

Gloxinia

Gloxinias are prized for their stunning trumpet-shaped flowers and lush, velvety foliage. Native to the rainforests of Brazil, these plants are popular choices for indoor gardens and decorative containers due to their vibrant colors and ease of cultivation.

Sunlight: Filtered sunlight or partial shade

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Plant in a lightweight potting mix rich in organic matter and repot anually. Keep the soil consistently moist. Mist the leaves regularly or place the plant on a humidity tray filled with water and pebbles to increase moisture around the plant. Keep it in warm temperatures.

Ixora

Ixora, also known as flame of the woods or jungle flame, is a tropical evergreen shrub prized for its vibrant clusters of small, tubular flowers and glossy foliage. Native to Asia and Africa, Ixora is a popular choice for adding color and beauty to gardens, landscapes, and indoor spaces.

Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Plant in a well-draining, slightly acidic soil. Keep the soil consistently moist. Do not expose to temperatures below 10° C.

Jasmine

Jasmine, with its delicate flowers and intoxicating fragrance, is a beloved flowering plant cherished for its beauty and aromatic presence. Originating from tropical and subtropical regions of Eurasia, jasmine has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value and its use in perfumery, culinary, and cultural practices.

Sunlight: Full sun to partial shade

Pet friendly: NO! Toxic if ingested!

Care and Other:
Plant jasmine in well-draining, fertile soil with good organic content. Water thoroughly when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch. Do not expose to temperatures below 10° C.

Orchid

Orchids are a diverse and fascinating group of flowering plants renowned for their exquisite blooms and elegant appearance. With over 25,000 species and countless hybrids, orchids are found in nearly every corner of the world. These plants have captured the imagination of enthusiasts and collectors for centuries, symbolizing beauty, love, and refinement.

Sunlight: Filtered sunlight or dappled shade

Pet friendly: Yes!

Care and Other:
Plant in an orchid mix and water thoroughly when the top inch of soil feels dry. Orchids prefer moderate temperatures during the day and cooler temperatures at night. Protect from extreme temperature fluctuations and drafts.

Peace Lily

The Peace Lily is a popular and elegant houseplant known for its lush, dark green foliage and striking white flowers. Originating from tropical regions of the Americas and Southeast Asia, Peace Lilies are valued for their ability to purify indoor air and their ease of care, making them a favorite choice for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.

Sunlight: Bright indirect light

Pet friendly: NO!!! Extremely toxic to pets! Even the pollen can cause fatal kidney failure for a cat or dog.

Care and Other:
Plant in a peat-based potting mix and repot every 1-2 years. Keep the soil moist. Peace Lilies will wilt dramatically when they need water but will quickly recover once watered. They thrive in high humidity, so mist the leaves regularly.
